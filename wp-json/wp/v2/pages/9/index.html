Recently someone asked me if I do “Yoga”. <\/p>\n\n\n\n

“No,” I finally said, “I certainly do NOT do Yoga.”<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I usually change the subject when someone brings up the dirty “Y” word. Normally, I do not explain<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Let me start with 2 stories.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I remember many years ago I bought a book called something like The Yoga Bible<\/em> (or similar). And the book told me, in all seriousness:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

\n

Yoga isn\u2019t just about exercise. You can also combine it with Buddhist style meditations<\/p>\n<\/blockquote>\n\n\n\n

And I was like, Yoga is<\/em> a type of meditation. Surely the author of the Yoga Bible knew that?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The 2nd story<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Many years ago, I was doing a Yoga class with Steve from the Yoga Sanctuary in Southampton. One of the students wanted Steve to sign a paper saying she had done an hour of Yoga.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Why?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Because she was doing a yoga teacher training course and needed to \u201cprove\u201d that she was doing X hours of yoga every week. Her instructor demanded it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Steve refused.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Later on, he said, \u201cI really need to stop this nonsense. If her teacher doesn\u2019t even trust her, why is he even bothering to teach yoga?\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n
