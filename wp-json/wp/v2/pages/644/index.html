This article comes from my own struggle in talking and teaching spirituality. I have written about this before, so won’t go over it again<\/a>, but this is like the 4th version of this site. I created and deleted 3 sites because I felt I wasn’t ready. I felt like a fraud, that people would discover I’m not qualified to talk about these things.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Funnily enough, I know about impostor syndrome. Have even written about it a few times. But I thought to myself: It isn’t impostor syndrome if it’s true, is it?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The reason I’m writing this article now: I was thinking about how Gurus go bad, and how so many have scandals. There are multiple reasons for that, and I will go into more detail into that in a later article. But while thinking about it, I realised something: While many people like me (and I know a few) are very hesitant about putting ourselves out there, there are 2 types of people who don’t have this problem:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/p>\n\n\n\n

1. Narcissist types: You know the gurus talking about love and peace, and then inviting attractive female students into the back room for “extra” practice? If you don’t know any, you haven’t spent much time in the community.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/p>\n\n\n\n

2. Religious fanatics: They never doubt or question themselves, because THEY HAVE THE TRUTH DAMMIT! And they will shove it down your throat whether you want it or not.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

<\/p>\n\n\n\n

And so we reach a situation where the only people talking about spirituality are the ones who have the least experience in it; in fact, those who are the most hostile and harmful to genuine inner spiritual experience. While people who have had inner spiritual experiences are sitting there with their doubts and Gee wizz I’m not good enough, better spend another 10 years meditating<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Spiritual Imposter Syndrome<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

I don’t know if anyone else has noticed this. I’m sure other people have, but I haven’t heard anyone talk about it much. And that is: Imposter Syndrome in Spiritual Circles.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Us spiritual types are told we have to be humble, quiet and forgiving. We are fed these heroic (and increasingly, it seems to me, bullshit) stories about ordinary Joes who go up mountains and return fully enlightened, their Kundalini risen, their Chakras open, and a “Fully Certified Enlightened Master Level 9000″<\/em> Certificate from the Association of Enlightened Gurus <\/em>(AEG).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But what if this view is wrong?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Enlightenment Line<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

We view enlightenment as a racing line– if you cross the line, you are enlightened. If you fail– then you might as well vote for Trump, as your life is ruined!<\/p>\n\n\n\n
