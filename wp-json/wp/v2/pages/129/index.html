Spiritual Materialism is a concept I found difficult to understand. It took me some time to understand it– there were some examples of things that irked me, and I only recently realised they are Spiritual Materialism.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What is Spiritual Materialism<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n

First, what is materialism? It is the belief that collecting more things– money, house, sex, status etc will make us happy. Most people think this is the goal of life.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But when we turn to spirituality, we bring this attitude to it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Suddenly, it’s like, If only I meditate more, I can become enlightened faster!<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

If only I buy these crystals\/go to that healer, my chakras will be opened quicker!<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

My guru is more enlightened than your guru!<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Definition<\/strong>: Here is my best attempt:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Spiritual Materialism is pursuing meditation or spiritual practices to gain external rewards or status or personal gains, and not for inner growth and self transformation<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Take the 1st example above, about meditating more to become enlightened quicker, like meditation is some sort of god damn competition. I’ve seen many people boast how much they meditate (and slyly humiliate others– “What, you only meditate 15 minutes daily? LOL that’s why you no enlightened, my man”<\/em>).<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This belief is so common that every meditation book \/ course assumes this–as well as every forum I’ve joined.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
